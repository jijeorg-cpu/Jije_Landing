Build a responsive, mobile-first one-page website for a startup called Jijé.

Purpose:
Create a clear, culturally grounded landing page that introduces Jijé as a marketplace for African food, vendors, and cultural experiences, and drives waitlist sign-ups ahead of launch.

Target audience:
African immigrants, diaspora communities, early users, vendors, event organizers, and partners.

Tone:
Warm, confident, culturally authentic, and modern. Avoid fintech language, hype, or technical jargon.

Brand direction:
- Primary color: deep green (earthy and cultural)
- Secondary colors: off-white and neutral gray
- Typography: modern rounded sans-serif (similar to Quicksand)
- Layout: clean, minimal, generous white space
- Avoid stock photos of people; use illustrations, food imagery, icons, or abstract cultural visuals

Page structure and content:

SECTION 1: Hero
- Headline: “Culture, Delivered.”
- Subheadline: “Discover African food, trusted vendors, and cultural events in your city.”
- Supporting text: “Jijé connects communities to the food and experiences that feel like home.”
- Primary CTA button: “Join the Waitlist”
- Secondary CTA link: “Explore What’s Coming”
- Visual: subtle cultural illustration or food-focused graphic

SECTION 2: The Problem
- Title: “Finding Home Isn’t Easy.”
- Bullet points:
  • Authentic African food is hard to find
  • Great vendors rely on social media and word of mouth
  • Cultural events are scattered and hard to discover
- Simple icons representing food, vendors, and community

SECTION 3: The Solution
- Title: “One Place for Culture and Community.”
- Three feature cards:
  1. Food & Groceries — “Shop from trusted African food vendors near you”
  2. Cultural Discovery — “Explore weekly food themes, stories, and traditions”
  3. Events & Concerts — “Find cultural events and concerts happening in your city”
- Minimal iconography or illustrations

SECTION 4: How It Works
- Title: “How Jijé Works”
- Step 1: “Browse African food vendors and cultural experiences”
- Step 2: “Order food or discover events near you”
- Step 3: “Enjoy culture — delivered or experienced live”
- Simple step-based layout

SECTION 5: Culture & Events Spotlight
- Title: “More Than Food — It’s Culture”
- Content:
  • Weekly culture spotlights (e.g., Ghana Week)
  • Featured vendors and signature dishes
  • Local cultural events and concerts
  • Discover food near events and events through food
- Carousel or card-style layout

SECTION 6: Early Stage / Pilot
- Title: “Launching City by City”
- Content:
  • Jijé is currently pre-MVP
  • Preparing for pilot launches in select cities
  • Focused on quality vendors and authentic experiences
- Simple roadmap or timeline visual

SECTION 7: Who It’s For
- Title: “Built for the Community”
- Bullet groups:
  • Customers — discover food and culture
  • Vendors — reach customers beyond social media
  • Event organizers — promote cultural experiences
- Simple icons for each group

SECTION 8: Final CTA
- Headline: “Be First When Jijé Launches”
- Supporting text: “Join the waitlist to get early access when we launch in your city.”
- Primary CTA button: “Join the Waitlist”
- Secondary CTA link: “Partner With Us”

Footer:
- Minimal footer with brand name only
- Optional text: “Built with community at the center.”

Functional requirements:
- All CTAs open a simple email waitlist form
- Smooth scrolling between sections
- Accessible contrast and readable font sizes
- Mobile-first design, optimized for desktop
